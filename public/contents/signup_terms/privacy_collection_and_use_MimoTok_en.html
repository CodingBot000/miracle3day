Collection and Use of Personal Information

Purposes of Collection and Processing

  1. The Company processes personal information for the following purposes. Personal information is not used for purposes other than those listed below. If the purposes change, the Company will obtain separate consent and take necessary measures in accordance with Article 18 of the Personal Information Protection Act.
    • Membership registration and management
      To verify intent to sign up; identify and authenticate users for membership services; maintain and manage membership status; prevent fraudulent use; provide notices; and handle complaints.
      The Company does not collect or use personal information of children under the age of 14.
    • Service provision and use of content
      To perform and manage contracts related to service provision; send contracts or invoices; provide content and personalized services; conduct identity verification and financial services for transactions; process purchases, payments, settlements, and deliveries; and collect receivables so that services can be used smoothly.
    • Inquiry response and complaint handling
      To verify the identity of complainants; confirm complaint details; contact and notify for fact‑finding; provide results; respond to inquiries; and collect customer suggestions.
    • New service development and personalized services
      To verify service effectiveness; compile statistics on frequency of access and service use; build a privacy‑protective service environment; and improve services based on service‑use records.
    • Marketing and advertising
      To provide promotional information and opportunities to participate in events; deliver services and ads tailored to demographic characteristics; and send event notices.
  2. The Company collects personal information in the following ways:
    • When a data subject consents to collection and directly enters information during membership registration or use of the services;
    • When personal information is provided by partner services or organizations;
    • During customer support interactions via Zendesk (customer support solution), the MimoTok app or website, e‑mail, or phone;
    • Through online or offline events;
    • By automatic collection via tools such as Google API Client, Amplitude, Clarity, Firebase, AppsFlyer, Braze (marketing automation), and Zendesk (customer support solution);
    • The Company may collect information about a data subject’s online activities across the website and connected devices throughout the services;
    • For this purpose, the Company may use analytics services of entrusted processors as in item (5) above.

Processing and Retention Period

  1. The Company processes and retains personal information until membership withdrawal, in accordance with the retention/use period prescribed by law or the consent obtained at the time of collection. The Company promptly destroys personal information when the processing purpose is achieved, the business is closed, the data subject requests destruction, or consent to collection/use is withdrawn. However, the following exceptions apply:

    1) Retention required by law

    Legal basis Items retained Retention period
    Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc. Records on contracts or withdrawal of offers 5 years
    Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc. Records on payments and supply of goods/services 5 years
    Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc. Records on consumer complaints or dispute resolution 3 years
    Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc. Records on labeling/advertising 6 months
    Protection of Communications Secrets Act Service login records 3 months
    Framework Act on National Taxes All books and evidentiary documents for transactions prescribed by tax laws 5 years

    2) Measures regarding inactive users

    Pursuant to the amendment to the Personal Information Protection Act (abolition of the Personal Information Validity Period System, Article 39‑6), effective November 24, 2023, the Company does not convert users into dormant accounts or separately store personal information solely because they have not used the services for one year or more.
